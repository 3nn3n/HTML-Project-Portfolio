Best Movies According to Nitesh Negi

My top 3 movies of all time

Pulp Fiction

*Pulp Fiction* is a landmark film in modern cinema, directed by Quentin Tarantino and released in 1994. Known for its nonlinear narrative, razor-sharp dialogue, and eclectic soundtrack, the movie interweaves multiple crime stories centered around Los Angeles. It features unforgettable performances by an ensemble cast, including John Travolta, Uma Thurman, and Samuel L. Jackson, whose portrayal of hitman Jules Winnfield delivered some of cinema’s most iconic lines. Blending dark humor, violence, and a stylized aesthetic, *Pulp Fiction* redefined independent filmmaking and earned the Palme d’Or at Cannes, solidifying Tarantino's status as a master storyteller. Its cultural impact and influence on storytelling remain profound, inspiring generations of filmmakers.

Schindler's List

*Schindler’s List*, directed by Steven Spielberg and released in 1993, is a powerful historical drama based on true events during the Holocaust. The film tells the story of Oskar Schindler, a German businessman who saved over 1,000 Jews by employing them in his factories. With remarkable performances by Liam Neeson, Ben Kingsley, and Ralph Fiennes, the film portrays the harrowing realities of the Holocaust while highlighting themes of courage and humanity. Shot in stark black and white, its raw visual style and emotional depth earned it seven Academy Awards, cementing it as one of the most significant films in cinematic history.

The Good, The bad & The Ugly

*The Good, the Bad and the Ugly* is a 1966 spaghetti Western directed by Sergio Leone, celebrated for its epic storytelling, iconic score by Ennio Morricone, and masterful use of tension. The film follows three gunslingers—played by Clint Eastwood, Lee Van Cleef, and Eli Wallach—on a quest to find buried treasure during the chaos of the American Civil War. Known for its sweeping cinematography, gritty characters, and legendary standoff finale, it redefined the Western genre, blending morality, greed, and survival in a timeless tale of intrigue and conflict.
